About the Maharishi Startup – “Hackathon 2.0” - 2023

The Maharishi Startup Hackathon is a program where students get a chance to solve everyday problems and come up with new ideas for products. It helps them become more creative and better at solving problems. In the "Hackathon 2.0" in 2023, students will work on challenges and provide solutions. This event takes place during the Maharishi Startup Mela 2023. It's a fast-paced competition that lasts for 8 hours and includes various challenges. Students from different colleges will participate and compete to become innovators or entrepreneurs. The event has two parts: one focuses on hardware, and the other on software.

Objectives of Events:

  • Foster Innovation Culture: Cultivate a culture of innovation among students through real-world problem-solving.
  • Problem-Solving Mindset: Develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills among participants.
  • Promote Entrepreneurship: Inspire entrepreneurship by encouraging innovative solutions to challenges.
  • Intercollegiate Competition: Facilitate healthy competition and networking among students from different colleges.
  • Multidisciplinary Collaboration: Encourage diverse perspectives and holistic problem-solving by organizing both hardware and software sections.

Rules and Regulations of MUIT HACKATHON 2023

  1. Registration for HACKATHON 2023

    Registration of teams must be done by a Team Leader only. The registration process is simple. All you need to do is:

    1. Visit https://forms.gle/8CVGC2vfjBhhWn236
    2. Fill in all the required details.
    3. Prepare a presentation after selecting the problem statement.
    4. Don’t forget to click ‘Submit’. You have been successfully registered.

  2. Team Formation Rules

    • Each team would comprise 2 to 6 members (including a team leader) with 1 team mentor (optional but recommended).
    • Team members could be from different institutions as well. Make sure you have the proper ID card for reference.
    • Teams should be made up exclusively of students who are not organizers, Hackathon team.
    • Teams must submit their project work on the theme as mentioned above only.
    • No plagiarism/copy-paste will be entertained; if found, the teams will directly be disqualified from the hackathon.
    • The hackathon begins on [Date], at [Time] and ends on [Date] and [Time].
    • The team must be registered for MUIT HACKATHON 2023 via Google forms provided to submit the hackathon.
    • Unregistered teams will not be entertained in any case.
    • The registered teams have to attend the introductory session via meet (details and link will be shared with the registered teams via emails).
    • The hackathon will be live just after the introductory sessions.
    • The submission form link will be shared with the registered teams, and the submission of PPT and other required files must be uploaded in the submission form.
    • Only one person in the team would be the team leader, who must register on behalf of all the members.
    • The PPT (4-5 Slides) of the idea and project link/application/software link for the hackathon should be submitted on the form provided along with all the mandatory details.
    • All the details should be provided in the prescribed format; otherwise, they are bound to get rejected.
    • Teams that have not submitted the hackathon will be automatically disqualified.
    • Already implemented hackathons can be used, but extra points will be given to original and innovative hackathons.

  3. Selection Criteria and Follow-ups

    • Evaluation criteria will include novelty of the hackathon, complexity, clarity, and details in the prescribed format, feasibility, practicability, sustainability, the scale of impact, user experience, and potential for future work progression.
    • Results of the Hackathon will be declared on [Date]. Results will be displayed on the website (link will be shared soon), and the shortlisted teams will be notified via email.

  4. General Hackathon Rules

    • All work on a project should be done during the Hackathon. Your code repository must be initialized at the beginning of the Hackathon itself.
    • Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
    • Teams must stop once the time is up. However, teams can debug and make minor fixes to their programs after the time is up.
    • Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers’ discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to breaking the Competition Rules, the Code of Conduct, or other unsporting behavior.
    • Hackathon Judging Criteria and presentation rules will be disclosed an hour before the Hackathon begins.
    • Valid bank account details must be provided when asked for the prize money to get processed. Regular processing time is applicable for the prize money to get processed.
    • In case of discrepancies, the final take shall lie with the organizers.
    • Last date of confirmation of participation by selected teams - Will be announced later
    • Last date of declaration of shortlisted candidates - Will be announced later

Judging Criteria:

  1. Design & Implementation:

    2. Does it work? Is it implementable? Did the team think about the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX)? Was the product designed with humans in mind?

  2. Innovation:

    3. How innovative/creative/unique is the idea? Was there a novel approach applied to solve the problem?

  3. Technical Complexity:

    4. Is the project technically impressive? Complex? How technically sophisticated/advanced/elegant was the solution?

  4. Social Impact:

    5. How impactful is the idea? Can it impact the lives of many people in a significant way? Is the idea aligned with and addresses any of the UN Sustainable Development Goals?

  5. Commercial Viability:

    6. Is there a business potential? How original is the business model? Has the team considered a route to market and made proactive actions to execute an action plan? Is the idea feasible, does it have economic and societal value, and is it sustainable?

List of Problem Statements:

  • Problem Statement 1: Smarter Surveillance System

    PS (Prelims): At present, a lot of man hours are spent on physical patrolling of border areas and compounds. Fencing wires are not reliable and easy to breach, in large areas it is difficult to be available at all locations, and if someone is breaching in absence of guard, guard will know only when they reach the breach site. Also, it takes a lot of time to cover the compound walls if the area is large.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Extend the technology to include advanced features or capabilities.

    Expectation: Develop a Technology that can address this issue partially or completely. The users of this system are two-fold: Any Security agency who is securing a larger area can install an unmanned area and can track through a single control room.

  • Problem Statement 2: Drone Technology

    PS (Prelims): Design heavy lift drones for disaster relief, capable of delivering essential supplies to remote areas.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Develop drone jammers to counter surveillance drones and integrate with mapping for pinpoint delivery.

    Expectation: Deliver a robust drone solution that can carry heavy loads, disrupt unauthorized drones, and ensure accurate delivery.

  • Problem Statement 3: Implementation of AI/ML in the Field of Education

    PS (Prelims): Utilize AI/ML models to address challenges in the education system, such as transportation, infrastructure, and teacher quality.

    Expectation: Create an AI/ML solution that enhances the overall quality and accessibility of education.

  • Problem Statement 4: IoT Based Smart Waste Management System for Smart City

    PS (Prelims): Design an IoT-based waste management system to prevent overflow in public bins.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Expand the system's capabilities for more comprehensive waste management.

    Expectation: Develop an efficient IoT solution for smart cities that improves waste management and public hygiene.

  • Problem Statement 5: Dark Web/Dark Net

    PS (Prelims): Develop a tool to search for keywords on the dark web with VPN connectivity.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Enhance the tool to expose IP details, track illegal activities, and gather comprehensive data.

    Expectation: Create a sophisticated tool for law enforcement to monitor, analyze, and respond to dark web activities effectively.

  • Problem Statement 6: Traffic/Crowd Management

    PS (Prelims): Create a smart traffic management system and provide real-time information to tourists.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Integrate advanced features like AI-driven traffic control and route diversion.

    Expectation: Deliver a system that ensures efficient traffic and crowd management for tourist destinations.

  • Problem Statement 7: News Alert/Flash

    PS (Prelims): Develop software to facilitate immediate communication among relevant authorities during incidents.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Expand the system to include geotagging, sentiment analysis, and comprehensive tracking of complaints.

    Expectation: Enable swift communication, response, and analysis of incidents to improve public safety.

  • Problem Statement 8: Road Safety

    PS (Prelims): Create smart pillars/sensors for monitoring vehicle speed and issuing automatic violations.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Implement a vehicle honk system to enhance safety in blind spots or curves.

    Expectation: Improve road safety through the deployment of smart sensors that monitor vehicle speed and promote safer driving.

  • Problem Statement 9: Intelligence

    PS (Prelims): Develop a tool to horizontally crawl and gather data from different platforms using email IDs or mobile numbers.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Extend data gathering to the deep and dark web for comprehensive profiling.

    Expectation: Enable law enforcement to efficiently gather information from various sources to develop profiles of suspected individuals.

  • Problem Statement 10: Open Ended Problem

    PS (Prelims): Develop a versatile tool or app to modernize police work.

    PS (Mains) Extension: Allow teams to explore and expand the tool's functionality as per their innovative ideas.

    Expectation: Deliver a state-of-the-art software/tool that enhances police efficiency and supports smart policing initiatives.
